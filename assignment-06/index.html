One Pot Wok Cashew Chicken

Serves 4

This is a great recipe to make quickly and have enough eat for leftovers. It's also great for head colds; it combines comfort food and enough spice to unblock a stuffy nose. If you add 2 full cups of vegetables, you may have to adjust the oyster sauce to taste to account for the added bulk. Note: do not touch eyes, nose, or open wounds after handing jalapenos.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken, cubed, salted, & peppered
  • 1 onion, sweet or white, chopped
  • 2-3 jalape√±os, de-seeded if heat intolerant, sliced thin
  • 1-2 cups chopped vegetable of choice (optional; this can provide healthy bulk to the meal if desired)
  • 1 cup cashews
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce, or to taste
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil

Prep:

  1. If serving with rice, start the rice.
  2. Add 1 tbsp oil to wok over high heat. Stirfry cashews over high heat for no more than 1.5 minutes. Remove from heat.
  3. Combine cooked cashews with honey and pepper flakes. Set aside.
  4. Add 1 tbsp vegetable oil to wok, allow oil to heat. Add chicken and stirfry ~5 minutes, till cooked.
  5. Remove chicken from wok, set aside.
  6. Add 1 tbsp oil to wok, add oinions, jalapenos, and chopped vegetables. Stirfry until densest vegetable is almost cooked.
  7. Add chicken back to wok and stirfry for 1-2 minutes.
  8. Add chicken stock and oyster sauce and stir to combine.
  9. Add cashew mixture to wok, stir to combine. Serve over white rice.
